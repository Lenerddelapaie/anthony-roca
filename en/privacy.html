Privacy policy

This page describes, in plain terms, what data is collected on this site, what it is used for, and how you can exercise your rights over your own data. It complies with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR, EU regulation of April 27, 2016).

In case of discrepancy between this English version and the French original, the French version prevails for legal purposes under French law.

General principle

This site collects as little data as possible. It does not use advertising trackers, does not share any data with data brokers, and does not profile its visitors. The only personal data that may be collected is your email address — and only if you freely choose to subscribe to the newsletter.

This restraint is an editorial choice, consistent with the posture of the site.

Data collected

When subscribing to the newsletter, the site collects only:

  • Your email address
  • The subscription date
  • Optional: your name, if you provide it voluntarily

When making a contribution (sending a case, a contradiction, or a contact message), the site collects:

  • The information you choose to share in your message
  • Your email address, so that I can reply to you

When simply visiting the site, the following technical data may be recorded by the host (Cloudflare) for security and performance purposes:

  • IP address (pseudonymized)
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • Referring site

This technical data is not associated with your identity and is used solely for aggregated operational purposes.

Purposes

Your data is used exclusively to:

  • Send you the site newsletter (if you have subscribed)
  • Reply to you when you reach out
  • Publish your contribution on the site, with your consent, under the conditions set out in the legal notice (in French)
  • Ensure the proper functioning and security of the site

Your data is never used for commercial prospecting, targeted advertising, or profiling.

Retention period

Newsletter email address: kept as long as you remain subscribed. Deleted immediately upon unsubscription.

Contact messages: kept for a maximum of three years after the last exchange, then deleted.

Published contributions: kept as long as the contribution remains published on the site. Deleted if you request the removal of your contribution.

Host technical data: kept according to Cloudflare's policy (typically 30 days).

Recipients

Your data is never shared with any third party for commercial purposes. It may be technically processed by the following subprocessors, chosen for their GDPR compliance:

Site hosting: Cloudflare, Inc. (United States), via the Cloudflare Pages service. Cloudflare is certified compliant with the European Standard Contractual Clauses for data transfers outside the EU.

Newsletter service: to be specified once the service is activated. The chosen provider will be GDPR-compliant, with European hosting or equivalent compliance.

Professional email: Google Workspace (Google LLC, United States), under European contract.

None of these subprocessors has the right to use your data for any purpose other than what is strictly necessary for the service provided.

Cookies

This site uses no advertising or tracking cookies. It does not use Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or any other profiling tool.

The host (Cloudflare) may place strictly necessary technical cookies for the proper functioning of the site (for example, attack protection). These cookies do not require your prior consent under the ePrivacy directive, because they are indispensable to the service.

The site does not embed social networks that would place third-party cookies.

Your rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights over your personal data:

  • Right of access: to know what data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification: to correct inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure: to request deletion of your data
  • Right to restriction: to temporarily restrict the use of your data
  • Right to portability: to receive your data in a structured format
  • Right to object: to object to the processing of your data
  • Right to withdraw consent: at any time, without justification

To exercise these rights, write to anthony@la-paie-facile.com, specifying the right you wish to exercise. A reply will be sent within one month at the most.

Right to file a complaint

If you believe your rights are not being respected, you may lodge a complaint with the French data protection authority:

If you are based in another EU country, you may also lodge a complaint with your own national data protection authority.

Changes

This privacy policy may be updated to reflect technical or legal evolutions. The last update date appears at the bottom of this page. Substantial changes will be announced in the newsletter.
